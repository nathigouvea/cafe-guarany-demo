Our Story

A Heritage Carved
in Marble and Memory

On January 20th, 1933, Porto gained one of its most beloved institutions. Café Guarany opened its doors on Avenida dos Aliados, designed by architect Rogério de Azevedo and adorned with a striking marble relief of a South American Indian by sculptor Henrique Moreira.

The name pays homage to the Guaraní people of Paraguay, Brazil, and Uruguay, evoking the deep connection between South America and the world of coffee. Brazil, the planet's leading coffee producer, inspired the café's identity from the very beginning.

"A place where every cup carries a century of conversation, culture, and song."

Known affectionately as O Café dos Músicos (The Musicians' Café), Guarany became a sanctuary for Porto's intellectuals, artists, and performers. The interior features a stunning triptych by celebrated artist Graça Morais on the main wall, warm golden tones, and the timeless elegance of marble throughout.

Thoughtfully restored in 2003 under the same stewardship that brought Café Majestic back to life in 1994, Guarany continues to honour its legacy while welcoming each new generation of guests.
